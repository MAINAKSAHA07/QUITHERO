# The Five Core Beliefs

| ID | Belief | Assessed As | Target Days |
|----|--------|-------------|-------------|
| belief_relaxation | "Smoking genuinely relaxes me" | belief_relaxation score | Day 4, 17 |
| belief_enjoyment | "I genuinely enjoy smoking" | belief_enjoyment score | Day 3, 8 |

# Challenge Strategies

## belief_relaxation
- Core truth: Nicotine withdrawal creates the tension that the next cigarette "relieves"
- Evidence: Cortisol levels are HIGHER in smokers than non-smokers at baseline
- Reframe: "Smoking doesn't reduce stress — it creates withdrawal stress then briefly ends it"
- Key days: Day 4 (The Relaxation & Stress Myth), Day 17 (A Real Stress Toolkit)

## belief_enjoyment
- Core truth: "Enjoyment" is relief from withdrawal interpreted as pleasure
- Evidence: First cigarette was unpleasant; the body adapted to tolerate poison
- Reframe: "You don't enjoy smoking — you enjoy ending the discomfort of not smoking"
- Key days: Day 3 (The Pleasure Illusion), Day 8 (Nothing to Give Up)

## belief_concentration
- Core truth: Nicotine withdrawal causes poor concentration; smoking just reverses it
- Evidence: Non-smokers concentrate without nicotine; smokers could too after withdrawal passes
- Reframe: "Cigarettes don't help you think — they fix a problem they created"
- Key day: Day 5 (Concentration, Boredom & Confidence Myths)

## belief_social
- Core truth: Social confidence is a skill, not a chemical effect
- Evidence: Most social situations happen fine without smoking; it's identity/habit
- Reframe: "You're the same person with or without a cigarette in your hand"
- Key day: Day 14 (Other Smokers and Social Pressure)

## belief_stress_relief
- Core truth: Identical to relaxation belief — withdrawal creates the stress
- Evidence: Former smokers report lower stress than active smokers within 3 months
- Reframe: "Every cigarette guarantees future stress (withdrawal). Stopping ends the cycle."
- Key days: Day 4, 17, 27

# How Each Archetype Scores

## Stress Reactor
- belief_relaxation: typically 8–10 (highest of all archetypes)
- belief_stress_relief: typically 9–10
- Content strategy: Lead with cortisol science before reframing

## Escapist
- belief_enjoyment: typically 7–9
- belief_concentration: typically 6–8
- Content strategy: Lead with "what are you actually escaping?" before debunking

## Social Mirror
- belief_social: typically 9–10
- belief_enjoyment: typically 7–8
- Content strategy: Identity reframe before belief challenge

## Auto-Pilot
- All beliefs typically 4–6 (lower — habit not belief driven)
- belief_relaxation: typically 5–7
- Content strategy: Habit interruption before cognitive work
