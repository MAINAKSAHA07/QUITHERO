# Overview

The Auto-Pilot Smoker lights up without thinking — after meals, with coffee, in the car, at specific times. The core illusion: "It's just what I do." No strong emotional driver; pure behavioral conditioning. This makes the habit feel invisible but also makes it the most mechanically breakable with awareness tools.

# Content Adaptation

- **Tone**: Pattern-interruption focused, curiosity-driven, routine-hacking. Use concrete "instead of X, try Y" framing.
- **Priority modules**: Day 12 (Triggers & Association of Ideas), Day 13 (Coffee, Meals & Alcohol), Day 28 (Rewriting Old Associations).
- **Craving support**: Lead with awareness-based tools (the pause technique, counting breaths) before emotional processing.
- **Avoid**: Deep emotional exploration early on — this archetype responds to practical disruption, not introspection.

# Notification Strategy

- Best send window: 5–10 minutes BEFORE their routine smoking time (inferred from craving timestamps).
- Message style: Pattern-interrupt, playful, awareness-triggering. "About to light up automatically? Notice it. That's the win."
- Avoid: Heavy emotional language — keep it light and observational.

# Behavioral Signals That Confirm This Archetype

- Cravings at highly predictable times (same hour daily)
- HABIT trigger dominates (>60%)
- Low emotional intensity on cravings (1–3 range consistently)
- Even session completion pace (neither rushing nor lingering)
- Few journal entries (doesn't naturally introspect about smoking)
- High step completion rate (follows structured content well)
