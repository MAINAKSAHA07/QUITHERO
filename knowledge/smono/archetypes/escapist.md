# Overview

The Escapist uses nicotine to fill perceived emotional voids — boredom, loneliness, restlessness. The core illusion: "Smoking gives me something to do / makes me feel less alone." In reality, nicotine creates the restlessness it pretends to solve; the void was manufactured by the addiction cycle itself.

# Content Adaptation

- **Tone**: Warm, introspective, imagery-rich. Use metaphor and narrative. Avoid clinical coldness.
- **Priority modules**: Day 5 (Boredom Myth), Day 18 (Boredom & Restlessness), Day 22 (Moment of Revelation).
- **Craving support**: Lead with visualization and curiosity-based approaches before action steps.
- **Avoid**: Overly structured checklists during introspective moments. This archetype responds to narrative, not bullet points.

# Notification Strategy

- Best send window: Evening hours (loneliness peaks), or mid-afternoon lulls.
- Message style: Reflective, gentle, possibility-focused. "What if the empty feeling was never yours?"
- Avoid: Action-urgent framing ("Do this NOW!") — feels pushy to this archetype.

# Day-Specific Insertions

| Day | Insertion |

# Behavioral Signals That Confirm This Archetype

- App opens clustered in evening hours (7–11 PM)
- Cravings logged with BOREDOM trigger > 50% of total
- Longer session completion times (savors content)
- Journal entries mention loneliness, emptiness, "nothing to do"
