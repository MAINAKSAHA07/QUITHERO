# Overview

The Social Mirror smokes because smoking is woven into their social identity. The core illusion: "I can't socialize without smoking" or "Smoking connects me to people." The truth: non-smokers socialize perfectly well; smoking isolates you from the majority who don't smoke.

# Content Adaptation

- **Tone**: Community-focused, identity-affirming, belonging-oriented. Use "we" language where appropriate.
- **Priority modules**: Day 14 (Other Smokers & Social Pressure), Day 13 (Coffee, Meals & Alcohol), Day 28 (Rewriting Old Associations).
- **Craving support**: Reframe social situations as opportunities to practice the new identity.
- **Avoid**: Isolating language ("you need to stay away from smokers"). This archetype needs connection, not avoidance.

# Notification Strategy

- Best send window: Before known social events (evening/weekend hours when social smoking peaks).
- Message style: Identity-affirming, future-self focused. "You're the one in the group who chose freedom."
- Avoid: Shaming social behavior or implying their friends are bad influences.

# Behavioral Signals That Confirm This Archetype

- Cravings cluster on weekends and evenings (social hours)
- SOCIAL trigger dominates craving logs (>50%)
- App engagement drops on weekends (socially busy)
- Journal entries mention friends, going out, "everyone else was smoking"
- Lower overall craving count but intensity spikes in social contexts
