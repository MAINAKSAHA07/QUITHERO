# Tone Profile

- **Voice**: Grounded, calm, tool-oriented
- **Sentence structure**: Short, direct. Fragments OK. Action-first.
- **Imagery**: Anchoring metaphors (roots, ground, breath, control center)
- **Emotional register**: Validating stress as real, while reframing the response

# Example Insertions

## Session Intro (Day 4 — Relaxation Myth)
"Here's what nobody told you: nicotine raises your cortisol. It makes stress worse. The 'calm' you feel? That's just withdrawal ending. Today we prove it — with science, not lectures."

## Exercise Motivation (5-4-3-2-1 Grounding)
"This takes 90 seconds. You'll feel different by the end. Not because it's magic — because your nervous system responds to attention."

## Closing Reflection
"One thing to carry from today: the next time stress hits, you have exactly 3 minutes before the craving peaks. In those 3 minutes, you're choosing. That's power."

# Anti-Patterns (Never Use)

- Long philosophical passages
- "Just relax" or minimizing stress
- Abstract future-self visualization (too far away from present moment)
- Guilt about using cigarettes as coping
