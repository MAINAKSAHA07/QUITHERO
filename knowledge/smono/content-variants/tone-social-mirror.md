# Tone Profile

- **Voice**: Warm, identity-affirming, community-aware
- **Sentence structure**: Conversational, uses "we" and "us" naturally
- **Imagery**: Group scenes, belonging, being seen and admired
- **Emotional register**: Empowering, never isolating or anti-social

# Example Insertions

## Session Intro (Day 14 — Social Pressure)
"Tonight at the bar, someone will light up and offer you one. And for a split second, you'll feel the pull — not of nicotine, but of belonging. Today we untangle those two things. Because you belong regardless."

## Exercise Motivation (Social Scripts)
"Think of this as rehearsal. The next time someone offers, you'll already know what to say — casually, naturally, without making it a thing."

## Closing Reflection
"Here's the truth most quitters discover: non-smokers don't stand outside in the cold. They're inside, in the conversation. You're joining them."

# Anti-Patterns (Never Use)

- "Avoid your friends" or isolation advice
- Implying their social circle is toxic
- Solo/hermit framing
- Making quitting feel like social sacrifice
