# The Smono Reset Method

## Philosophy

You are not broken. You are not weak. You are a rational person caught in an irrational trap. The trap has three components:
1. **Physical addiction** — nicotine withdrawal (mild, peaks at 72 hours, gone in 2 weeks)
2. **Psychological conditioning** — learned associations between cigarettes and situations
3. **Belief system** — "I enjoy smoking," "It relaxes me," "I can't cope without it"

The Reset Method works by dismantling #3 FIRST (Days 1–9), experiencing #1 with full understanding (Days 10–14), and reprogramming #2 through behavioral experiments (Days 15–30).

## Key CBT Principles Applied

- **Psychoeducation before change**: Understand the mechanism before attempting to change the behavior
- **Functional analysis (A→B→C)**: Map antecedent → behavior → consequence for every smoking episode
- **Cognitive restructuring**: Challenge and replace irrational beliefs about smoking
- **Behavioral experiments**: Test predictions ("I can't enjoy coffee without smoking") against reality
- **Exposure with response prevention**: Face triggers without smoking, proving survival
- **Relapse prevention**: Plan for high-risk situations BEFORE they happen

## The "Keep Smoking" Instruction (Days 1–9)

Unique to this method: users DO NOT quit on Day 1. They continue smoking while studying the trap. This:
- Removes performance anxiety ("What if I fail?")
- Allows real-time observation of their own smoking
- Builds cognitive dissonance (seeing the trap while still in it)
- Creates natural momentum toward Day 10 (the Reset Moment)

## Day 10: The Reset Moment

Reframed as liberation, not sacrifice. The user's final cigarette is a conscious, observed experience — not a dramatic goodbye. Key reframe: "You are not GIVING UP smoking. You are GETTING RID of it."

## Post-Reset (Days 11–30)

Physical withdrawal is addressed honestly (mild, manageable) while the real work focuses on:
- Breaking situational associations
- Handling social pressure
- Building genuine stress coping tools
- Guarding against "just one" thinking
- Establishing lifelong non-smoker identity
