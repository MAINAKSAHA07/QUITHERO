Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

CBT identifies and restructures the irrational beliefs that make smoking feel necessary. It doesn't fight the craving — it removes the reason for it.

🔍

Functional Analysis

Every craving follows an A→B→C pattern: Antecedent, Belief, Consequence. Once you see the pattern, you can interrupt it at the thought level.

⚡

Neural Pathway Rewiring

Repeated cognitive challenges weaken the neural associations between triggers and smoking. Within 10 days, the automatic response begins to fade.

Note: CBT works by changing the meaning attached to the craving. This app is educational and supportive, not a replacement for medical care.
